T/F It is appropriate to use 差[さ]し上[あ]げる to replace あげる in the ～てあげる grammar pattern (doing something for someone). Justify your answer (i.e. also recall the right/wrong version of the sentence structure).	"False. They argue that the idea that you are doing a service for somebody is ultimately an insolent belief and that trying to talk humbly about it is a rather unconvincing facade. Such speakers prefer instead to use the “お ＋ stem ＋ する” pattern.  Instead of: 私は先生に地図を見せてさしあげました。 Use: 私は先生に地図をお見せしました。 I (humbly) showed a map to my professor"""									
What is the general formula for humble expression?	General formula for humble expressions お＋verb stem + する e.g. 先生に本をお貸しするつもりです (I intend to (humbly) lend the professor my book.)									
What is the ～ないで grammar structure used to express?	without doing x... 昨日の夜は、寝ないで、勉強しました (Last night, I studied without getting any sleep)									
"What is the general formula for ""without doing x""?"	verb (short, negative) + で 昨日の夜は、寝ないで、勉強しました (Last night, I studied without getting any sleep)									
"How would you express longer sentences that represent the idea ""I don't know"" or ""whether or not""?"	use of wh-word + か 昨日何を食べた**か**、覚えていません。 What I ate yesterday, I don't remember.  yes/no question + かどうか 進学するかどうか、まだ決めていません。 Whether I should advance to tertiary studies, I have not decided.

How would you describe something that is psychologically easy to do/hard to do	～やすい・～にくい 本は読みやすいです (the book was easy to read) 魚は食べにくいです (fish are hard to eat)									
What is the only time you cannot use たら as an if conditional?	The only time you cannot use this is if A is not related to B. (e.g. 北海度に行ったら、６月がいいですよ is not correct. this impliesてから, and that B has already occured ('june is good' is not a phrase that can occur).)									
"What is the ONLY ""if"" conditional that can be used for ""if A, then B"", given A and B are not related, and/or B is something in the future."	なら ( e.g. 北海度に行くなら、６月がいいですよ) (If you are going to Hokkaido, June is good)									
What are the cases where you cannot use the と conditional, given AとB?	B cannot represent commands or will (cannot use ～ね(e.g. 電話してね), volitionary form, or ～verb stem + なさい).
