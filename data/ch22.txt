What do causative sentences express?	Describes who makes someone do something, or who lets someone do something.  e.g. 先生は学生に会話を覚えさせました The professor made the students memorise the dialogue						
What is the conjugation for causative form?	conjugation  五段　動詞 う＞あ＋せる 行く＞行かせる 買う＞買わせる  一段　動詞 動詞 + させる 食べる＞食べさせる  irregular する＞させる 来[く]る＞来[こ]させる						
What is the general structure for causative sentences when describing a person makes someone do something?	director は cast に action 先生 (director) は学生 (cast) に会話を覚えさせました (action) The professor made the students memorise the dialogue						
Given the following structure for causative sentences director は cast に action What are the two cases where を is used instead?	in cast に, there are cases where を is used instead of に  1) when the action is caused by reflex, like laughing or crying.    e.g. 私はその子供を泣かせてしまいました。    I accidentally made that child cry.  2) when the verb turned into causative didnt originally call for を    e.g. 先生は私をトイレに行かせました    The teacher let me go to the toilet    X 両親は私を本を読ませました。    My parents let me read the book    The above answer is invalid because を is already used in 本を読む
