What is passive form used for?	to express inconvenience/bothersome of yourself when an event has occured. e.g. a friend of mine used my car 友達が車を使いました。 I had my car used by a friend (and I am mad/sad about it) 私は友達に車を使われました。						
"What is the formula for using passive form given that there is some sort of ""villain"" that caused trouble for you?"	victim は villain に evil act where evil act represents the passive verb e.g. I had my car used by a friend (and I am mad/sad about it) 私は (victim) 友達 (villain) に車を使われました (evil act)。						
What are the verb conjugation rules for passive form?	五段/う 動詞 (godan) う＞あ＋れる 行く＞行かれる  一段/る 動詞 (ichidan) 動詞＋られる 食べる＞食べられる  irregular する＞される 来る＞来[こ]られる						
what are the two cases where you can't use passive form when expressing inconveniences/negative emotions of yourself?	"when the negative event in question is in form of an adjective (e.g. bad (悪い)) e.g. you cannot say ""I was annoyed by the fact that the weather is bad"" but you can say ""I was annoyed by the fact that it is raining""  when the verb itself is already negated. e.g. you cannot say ""Professor Yamashita was annoyed by the fact that his students did not come to his class"""						
T/F all passive form conjugations represent a negative meaning. (excluding inanimate objects) (elaborate why)	False. There are a few verbs whose passive form represents a neutral form instead of a negative form e.g. being asked out by someone, being introduced to someone, being looked up by someone (デートに誘われました、友達に紹介されました、みんなに尊敬[そんけい]されています)						
What is the ～てある structure used to represent?	used to bring up things that are done on purpose (though the person who did said action is unknown) e.g.机の上に花が置いてあります。 Flowers were put on the table (and they have remained there ever since)

What does A間にB represent?	B taking place in the middle of event A. e.g. 留守 (B) の間に友達が来ました (A) (my friend came while I was out)						
True or False Given A間にB for verbs, ている is usually used for event A, even if event A is in the past.	True e.g. お風呂に入っている間に電話がありました There was a call while I was taking a bath.  Note: in this context, ている would describe state.						
what is the difference between A間B and A間にB?	If event B extends beyond event A (i.e. event B continues after event A ends), 間 is used instead of 間に For 間に, B must be of short duration and start and end within the timeframe of event A.						
What does 形容詞 (adjective) + する represent?	to make something (adjective) e.g. 冷たくする (to make something colder)						
What does ～てほしい express/represent?	used for expressing private predicates (i.e. only representing the speaker's wishes) e.g. 私は妹に部屋を片付けてほしい (I wish my sister would clean up the room)						
what is the conjugation formula for 形容詞 (adjective) + する?	形容詞 (adjective) drop い, + くする 冷たくする よくする drop な, + にする 簡単にする  (note: conjugate it like you would with adjective + なる, but replace なる with する)						
when negating ～てほしい, what are the two possible ways you could do it?	for negations (i.e. things you don't want to do), making て形 verb into ない形 or negating ほしい into ほしくない are both fine. e.g. 昔の話をしてほしくない (I dont want to talk about the old times) 英語で話さないでほしい (I don't want to speak in english)						
What would you have to do when you use ～てほしい when describing other people?	if you want to use it for other people, you would have to use と or がっている, replacing the い in ほしい.  e.g. 先生は「学生にたくさん勉強してほしい」と言っています (「」is added for quotations emphasis. You don't have to include them.) Our professors say they want their students to study a lot  先生は学生にたくさん勉強してほしがっています Our professors (are acting in a way that suggests that they) want their students to study a lot
