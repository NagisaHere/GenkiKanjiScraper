what is the two meanings of ~てしまう？	1. action is done with determination (to completion) e.g. 本を読んでしまいました。 2. lack of premediation/control over how things turned out (with a sense of regret/unintention). e.g. 電車の中でかばんを忘れてしまいました。									
what is てしまう usually contracted to?	てしまう＞ちゃう でしまう＞じゃう									
sentence structure for when describing when A happens, b happens too	clause A と clause B									
describing two actions that occur at the same time	verb (stem) + ながら + verb 2 e.g. 音楽を聞きながら掃除します。

changing to ば form	う(ごだん) verbs い＞え, add ば e.g. 買えば  る（いちだん）verbs add れば e.g. たべれば  irregular: する＞すれば くる＞くれば									
negative version of ば form	ない形 (drop い), add ければ e.g. 食べなければ しなければ

Conjugation for と conditional (verb, i-adj, na-adj, noun)	辞書形＋と い形＋と な形　な becomes だ＋と noun + だ+ と									
become なるwith adjectives	い becomes くなる e.g. 遅くなる  な becomes になる e.g. 綺麗になる									
when is the と conditional usually used?	"When ""If A then B"" is ALMOST ALWAYS the case."									
t/f can you use ながら to describe two people doing things at the same time?	no. both actions in ながら must be performed by the same person.									
"Given A ながらB Which action is the ""main action""?"	B									
What is the idea around ば conditional?	When given a decision between two things (e.g. A and NOT A), choose one of the two. These mostly apply to things that are in your control.  e.g. The decision between dropping the pen, and holding the pen.
